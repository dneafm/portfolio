## DJ Trade

### Card title
DJ Trade

### Card summary
Built a trading operations interface for monitoring, reconciliation, and clearer execution under uncertainty.
DJ Trade is where new strategies are forged, tested, and turned into operational workflows.

### Case study headline
Built a trading operations surface for monitoring, reconciliation, and execution clarity.

### Intro
DJ Trade started as an attempt to make dense trading activity easier to monitor, review, and act on. I treated it less like a passive dashboard and more like an operational surface: what needs attention now, what changed, what is degraded, and what decision should happen next.

### Problem
Trading and portfolio activity tends to fragment across positions, signals, reports, and execution states. The real problem is not lack of data, but operator overload: too many moving parts, unclear priority, and weak visibility into what deserves attention first.

### Approach
I focused on turning reports into action-oriented workflows instead of static logs. That meant designing for scanability, comparison, degraded-state awareness, and preflight confidence before execution.

### What I did
- Turned reporting into a prioritized review surface
- Made live context easier to scan under pressure
- Added comparison and drill-down paths for decision support
- Surfaced degraded states and readiness checks to improve operator trust
- Framed DJ Trade as the place to forge and test new strategies, not just monitor existing ones

### Outcome
The result moved the experience away from a generic market dashboard and toward a decision-support tool. Its value is not just visual polish, but the way it structures noisy trading context into clearer operational judgment.

### Proof bullets
- Designed for action, not passive viewing
- Made fragmented trading context easier to compare and review
- Added visibility into degraded states, readiness checks, and follow-up paths
- Framed reporting as an operational workflow rather than a static output

## AgentBoard / HumanBoard

### Card title
AgentBoard / HumanBoard

### Card summary
Built a board-native decision system that turns noisy notes, ideas, and signals into reviewable action.

### Case study headline
Built a board-native decision system for turning messy information into prioritized action.

### Intro
AgentBoard and the HumanBoard direction came from a simple frustration: useful ideas, notes, and opportunities often die in fragments. I wanted a system that did more than store information. It needed to turn scattered inputs into review loops, clearer prioritization, and action without forcing everything into fake urgency.

### Problem
Knowledge tools often collect too much and decide too little. Backlogs become archives, research turns into noise, and long-horizon goals either disappear or get flattened into the same daily list. The same fragility shows up in AI workflows too: when task context and decisions live only inside scattered chats or one vendor layer, the operating thread can break as soon as the tool changes.

### Approach
I designed AgentBoard as a shared operational layer rather than another note container. It moves tasks, context, blockers, proofs, and handoffs out of fragile chat state and into a board-native system, with separate lanes for active work, bridge paths, and far-future watches. The goal was lower-noise prioritization, not endless backlog growth.

### What I did
- Turned scattered ideas into structured, reviewable work
- Built separate lanes for active work, bridge paths, and far-future watches
- Designed low-noise review loops instead of constant backlog churn
- Kept long-horizon direction visible without creating false urgency

### Outcome
The result behaves more like an operating surface than a note archive. Its value is in helping a person or team decide what matters now, what should be tested soon, and what should stay on a lower-frequency watchlist until the evidence changes.

### Proof bullets
- Turned knowledge clutter into reviewable action
- Designed prioritization around revenue, leverage, optionality, and evidence
- Kept far-future goals visible without creating false urgency
- Built for judgment-heavy workflows rather than simple task logging
